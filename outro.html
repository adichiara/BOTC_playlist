“Pleased to meet you / Hope you guess my name.” If Blood on the Clocktower had a theme song, this might be it. There’s no hiding the relevance here—this track is the game. With a charismatic narrator who revels in chaos, false charm, and centuries of mischief, "Sympathy for the Devil" doesn’t just hint at deceit—it luxuriates in it.

The song introduces its speaker as a charming figure who’s been pulling strings behind the scenes throughout history. “I’ve been around for a long, long year / Stole many a man’s soul and faith”—this could just as easily be the Demon on night one, smiling from the shadows. Or the devilish Minion who gently nudges suspicion in the wrong direction.

What elevates this song is its gleeful embrace of duplicity. There's no shame here, just cunning and confidence—traits that any good Clocktower antagonist must master. The line “Just as every cop is a criminal / And all the sinners saints” plays right into the reversal of roles, the blurred lines, the masked intentions that define every session of the game.

With a flawless 100% win rate, this isn't just the top-scoring song—it’s a crowning embodiment of what makes Blood on the Clocktower so captivating: the charm of darkness, the thrill of pretending, and the power of perception. This is the Devil you suspect—and admire.
